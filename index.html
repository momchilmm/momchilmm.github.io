Doubly-degenerate photonic crytal cavity
We have designed a microscopic photonic crystal resonator that confines light at a given wavelength and at half that wavelength. The device strongly enhances second-order optical nonlinear frequency conversion processes, which find applications in both classical and quantum technologies. Photonic crystal cavity, have always been thought of as an attractive candidate for this problem, but it turned out really hard to design one that confines light at two different wavelengths that are so far apart from each other. I first tried to do this in 2014 and couldn't, and no one elese managed since either. Then, we recently had a breakthrough idea based on bound states in the coninuum, which allowed us to achieve the feat.
Momchil Minkov, Dario Gerace, and Shanhui Fan
